British Football Academy has teamed up with the Employees Welfare Association (EWA) of the US Embassy to bring soccer classes to the Mitsui American Embassy Housing Compound. This 'Community Soccer programme', open to both EWA members and non-members, will run as a stand alone programme, slightly different regular BFA sessions. There will be 3 terms of 12 ~ 16 weeks, running Autumn, Winter and Spring and students will sign up per term.
Suitable for both boys and girls aged 3 years old to 8 years old, classes will be taught on the large grassy field within the Mitsui compound but moved inside the gym in case of inclement weather. Due to strict security at the housing compounds entry points, names of everyone attending classes must be given to the gate before the sessions, to permit entry for non EWA members.

Thursday Programs to be held on the Mitsui Compound Grass Area (or Gym in case of inclement weather or darkness): Autumn/Fall term = 5th September ~ 19th December 2013 (16 weeks)

Pups class 3~4 year olds: Session time: 3:10-3:40 PM.
The Pups Soccer class is a fantastic introduction to the game of soccer that our youngest boys & girls can enjoy together. This program has been designed to familiarize children with a ball and simple, fun games and exercises are used to develop motor and bipedal skills that will lead to players learning the very simplest techniques. 3-year olds can’t fail to enjoy these sessions - it’s been tried and tested! .

Bulldogs class for 5~6 year olds: Session times 3:50-4:40 PM.
Each 50-minute session is dedicated to introducing and reinforcing the essentials of ball control, passing, dribbling and shooting by way of fantastic games, many of which have been developed by our coaching staff specifically for this age group. Elements of competition will be introduced, but the emphasis is strictly on fun and positive participation - this builds confidence and will provide each child with a real sense of achievement at the end of each session.

Lions class for 7~8 year olds: Session times 4:50-5:50 PM.
This 1 hour, progressive skill development program focuses on ball control, dribbling, running with the ball, turning and shooting skills and techniques. A myriad of high-energy drills and games will be used to introduce and practice these fundamentals - all fun and all focused on a particular objective or combination thereof. Children will relish the micro soccer games that provide a competitive arena for them to demonstrate what they’ve learnt, although effort, positive peer interaction and teamwork are the true fundamentals of this dynamic program.
